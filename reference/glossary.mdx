---
title: "Glossary"
description: "Key terms used in the Raiders Depth Fragility app and docs."
---

## Availability at pick

The deterministic estimate that a prospect can be selected at a target pick. Already drafted prospects receive `0`.

## Cross-training cushion

Credit given when the roster has a player who can preserve a fragile role from another position. A C/G swing player reducing center fragility is the main example.

## Depth dropoff

The expected loss in role quality from the starter or primary role player to the next available option.

## Fragility

The likelihood that a position-group injury causes the Raiders to lose a meaningful part of the current scheme.

## Fragility reduction value

The prospect score shown in the table. It combines position fragility, role fit, readiness, availability, NFL IQ-style signal, and opportunity cost.

## NFL IQ signal

The normalized `nflIqOverall` value. The seed data uses public fallback values and the CSV workbench supports manual NFL IQ-style imports.

## Opportunity cost

A penalty for spending a pick earlier than a prospect's projected range or far away from his consensus board value.

## Position group

The fixed model categories: QB, RB, WR, TE, OT, IOL/C, EDGE, IDL, LB, CB, S, and ST.

## Readiness

How much a prospect can help within the current roster window. It is represented as a 0 to 1 multiplier.

## Replacement scarcity

How difficult it is to find the same role in the current market or draft window.

## Role fit

How directly a prospect's traits solve a position group's failure modes.

## Scheme dependency

How much the Raiders' current offensive, defensive, or special teams scheme depends on a role functioning well.

## Starter leverage

How much one player or primary role controls the position group.
