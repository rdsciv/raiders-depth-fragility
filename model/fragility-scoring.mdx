---
title: "Fragility scoring"
description: "The deterministic formula that ranks Raiders position groups by scheme fragility."
---

Fragility scoring estimates how much the Raiders lose if a starter or primary role player becomes unavailable.

The implementation is in:

```text
src/lib/scoring/fragility.ts
```

## Formula

```text
FragilityScore.total =
  0.35 * schemeDependency
+ 0.30 * depthDropoff
+ 0.20 * starterLeverage
+ 0.15 * replacementScarcity
- crossTrainingCushion
```

The total is rounded to one decimal and clamped at zero.

## Components

| Component | Scale | Meaning |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `schemeDependency` | 0 to 100 | How much the current scheme depends on that role |
| `depthDropoff` | 0 to 100 | How far the team falls if the starter is unavailable |
| `starterLeverage` | 0 to 100 | How much one player or role controls the position group |
| `replacementScarcity` | 0 to 100 | How hard it is to replace that role in the current market or draft |
| `crossTrainingCushion` | 0 to 40 | Credit for credible flex players or emergency role coverage |

## Why cross-training is subtracted

The tool is built around the idea that a team can reduce fragility without always drafting the nominal position. A guard who can survive at center can reduce center fragility. A safety who can play dime linebacker can reduce linebacker fragility. A tackle who can cover both sides reduces OT fragility.

That cushion is subtracted because it protects the scheme from a single-point failure.

## Example

```ts
const rawTotal =
  0.35 * role.dependencyScore +
  0.3 * depth.depthDropoff +
  0.2 * role.starterLeverage +
  0.15 * role.replacementScarcity -
  depth.crossTrainingCoverage;
```

## What high scores mean

A high score means all or most of these are true:

- The role is central to the current Raiders scheme.
- The starter carries unusual leverage.
- The backups do not preserve the same traits.
- Similar players are scarce.
- Cross-trained alternatives are limited.

## What low scores mean

A lower score does not mean a position is unimportant. It means the roster has more ways to survive an injury there, or the scheme can redistribute the workload without changing its identity.

<Warning>
Do not compare scores across seasons or staffs. The app has no season mode by design.
</Warning>
