---
title: "Interpret recommendations"
description: "How to use the recommendation panel without confusing fragility value for a full scouting grade."
---

Recommendations are not a replacement for scouting, medical, character, or trade value work. They answer a narrower question: which available prospect most reduces current Raiders scheme fragility at the active picks?

## Recommended use

Use the ranking as a second board beside the room's scouting board:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start from the highest fragility groups">
    Identify which positions would most damage the call sheet if a starter were unavailable.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check the prospect overlay">
    Look for players whose traits match those specific failure modes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Compare against the scouting board">
    A high fragility score should elevate a player within a tier, not erase scouting risk.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pressure-test the opportunity cost">
    If a prospect is projected to be available later, the model subtracts a reach penalty.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Why a lower-ranked prospect can win

A lower consensus prospect can outrank a higher consensus prospect when all of these are true:

- The Raiders are more fragile at the lower-ranked player's position.
- The player has a better role fit for that specific failure mode.
- The player is likely enough to be available at the target pick.
- The player has enough readiness to matter during the current roster window.

This is the core idea of the tool. The highest big-board player is not always the highest current team leverage player.

## What each recommendation means

### Best pick for Super Bowl leverage

The prospect with the highest final `ProspectFitScore.total`.

Use this when the room wants the best single answer to current fragility.

### Best fragility hedge

The prospect with the highest raw `fragilityReduction`.

Use this when the room cares more about protecting a fragile scheme function than about pick-specific board cost.

### Best scheme fit

The prospect with the highest `roleFit`.

Use this when the scouting staff believes several players are similarly graded and wants the cleanest schematic translation.

### Avoid despite board value

A player with strong public board value but low Raiders-specific fragility value.

Use this to challenge automatic "best player available" thinking when the player does not solve a current Raiders problem.

<Note>
The model intentionally removes Fernando Mendoza from the recommendation set because he has already been selected.
</Note>
